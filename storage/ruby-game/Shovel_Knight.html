Shovel Knight is a 2014 action platformer video game developed and published by independent developer Yacht Club Games for the Nintendo 3DS, Wii U, and personal computer platforms. The game was released worldwide for the PC and in North America for the 3DS and Wii U on June 26, 2014. A date has yet to be announced for a European 3DS or Wii U release. Shovel Knight was directed by former WayForward Technologies member Sean Velasco and includes music composed by Jake Kaufman and Manami Matsumae. The game was released for Mac OS X on September 13, 2014[2] and for Linux on October 8, 2014[3]

Gameplay[edit]

Pre-release screenshot of Shovel Knight. The game features graphics inspired by 8-bit NES games.

Shovel Knight is a 2D side-scrolling platform game. The game features 8-bit graphics designed to resemble classic games. The color scheme used adheres to the Nintendo Entertainment System's original color palette, and includes visual touches such as parallax scrolling.[4] The player controls the eponymous Shovel Knight character, who wields a sharpened shovel that is used to attack enemies, destroy barriers, and dig up treasures. As a homage to the game DuckTales, the player can use the shovel as a pogo stick to bounce off obstacles or the heads of enemies.[5]

Plot[edit]

Prior to the game, adventurers Shovel Knight and Shield Knight fought alongside one another, journeying across the world and seeking treasure. However, when the two explore the Tower of Fate, the two fall to the dark magic power of a cursed amulet. When Shovel Knight reawakens, the Tower has been sealed and Shield Knight is nowhere to be seen. Grieving for his beloved, Shovel Knight gives up adventuring and secludes himself. However, during his absence, the Enchantress rises to power, spreading evil across the land. Upon hearing that the Enchantress has unsealed the Tower of Fate, Shovel Knight takes up his shovel again and ventures towards it, hoping to find and rescue Shield Knight. In order to do so, Shovel Knight must fight the eight members of "The Order of No Quarter," who have been dispatched by the Enchantress to impede him.[6]

Development[edit]

Shovel Knight is the first video game developed by Yacht Club Games, directed by Sean Velasco. Velasco has stated that the game draws heavy influence from NES games, specifically Castlevania III: Dracula's Curse, DuckTales, Super Mario Bros. 3, and the Mega Man titles. The game also draws influence from U.N. Squadron and Dark Souls.[7][8] The game was announced on March 17, 2013 alongside the launch of a Kickstarter campaign to fund development, with a minimum funding goal of $75,000. The campaign reached this goal in late March and went on to collect a total of $311,502, fulfilling all announced stretch goals for additional features, by its end on April 13.[4][9] The game was originally slated for release in September 2013, but was delayed into early 2014.[10] After various further delays,[11][12] Yacht Club Games announced on June 5 that the game would be released on June 26, 2014.[13]

Additional features earned through Kickstarter stretch goals include a four-player battle mode, a mission-based challenge mode, an additional mode that swaps all the characters' genders, and playable story campaigns for boss characters King Knight, Specter Knight, and Plague Knight. These features will be added as free updates to the game throughout the year.[14]

Marketing[edit]

To promote the Kickstarter, Yacht Club distributed copies of their initial Penny Arcade Expo demo to several prominent gaming personalities on YouTube, including Two Best Friends Play and Game Grumps.[15][16] Shovel Knight will also be appearing as a playable character in the Wii U and PC game Hex Heroes.[17]

Audio[edit]

Shovel Knight features a chiptune soundtrack composed by Jake Kaufman, with two contributions by Mega Man composer, Manami Matsumae. The game's soundtrack was released for download via Bandcamp on the same day as the game, as was a separate album featuring various arranged versions of the game's tracks.[18][19]

IGN editor Colin Moriarty awarded the game a 9.0 rating."Shovel Knight--in all of its old-school glory--is arguably the best game released so far in 2014."[23][citation needed] Many reviewers noted that the game managed to maintain the essence of retro games while still forging its own identity.[citation needed] On Metacritic, the game has an aggregate score of 88 for the Wii U version, and 85 for the PC version based on 11 and 38 reviews respectively.[20][22] The 3DS version is the highest rated, with a score of 92 based on 6 reviews.[21] Kotaku featured it in "The 22 Best-Reviewed Games That Don't Have Sequels (Yet)" in 2014 because of its high Metacritic rating.[24] During IGN's mid-year "Game of the Year Watch" Colin and other editors including Brian Altano stated that Shovel Knight was a legitimate pick for their game of the year vote.[citation needed] They also stated that indie games such as Shovel Knight were starting to rival Triple A games like Titanfall.[citation needed] Infendo.com gave it 4 1/2 stars, out of 5, calling it "a brilliant homage to a bygone era, yet an equally fresh, captivating and innovative game."[25] Ben "Yahtzee" Croshaw of Zero Punctuation, reviewed the game favorably, especially praising the storytelling through gameplay and the difficulty which reminded him of the way Dark Souls utilized these elements. He also noticed that while some people might "write [the game] off as 'nostalgia bait'", he has "never owned an NES, so if I liked Shovel Knight, it can't possibly be working on nostalgia alone". [26]

Including Kickstarter backers, Shovel Knight sold 180,000 copies within a month of the North American launch. 49,000 copies were sold on Wii U, 59,000 were sold on 3DS, and 66,000 were sold on Steam.[27]
