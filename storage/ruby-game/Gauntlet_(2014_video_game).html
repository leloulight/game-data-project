Gauntlet is a top-down hack and slash video game released on 23 September 2014 and is the latest game in the Gauntlet series. The game was developed by Arrowhead Game Studios and is being published by Warner Bros. Interactive Entertainment.[5][6][7] The game will be released for the SteamOS Linux operating system later in the year.[8][9]

Gameplay[edit]

The player takes the role of one of four races of hero, allowing up to four-player multiplayer co-op gameplay.[5][6][10] The dungeons to be explored are part defined maps and part procedurally generated.[5][6][10]

Gauntlet received positive reviews from critics. Aggregating review websites Metacritic gave the PC version a 69/100. [11] Gaming website Shacknews stated that "so far, it looks like Gauntlet properly captures much of the spirit that made the 1985 arcade game so much fun", while also stating that the game isn't very intuitive in terms of figuring out the unlocked mechanics as you progress through the game.[5] Chris Brown, for New Zealand website Gameplanet, stated "Gauntlet scratches an itch I didn't realise I had", finding it "simple, but not brainless" but also "fun as hell"; he also highlighted the user interface in their preview build as "awkward."[6] In comparison to the 30 year old original, Destructoid stated "the game isn't a true recreation; it's more Arrowhead's ideal take on it", finding that "while the content may feel sort of new, nothing's ever really that fresh with Gauntlet. And that's probably just the way that fans would want it."[18] Dave Rudden, from IGN, gave the game 6.8/10, praising its 4-players co-op while criticizing the repetitive gameplay and low replay value.[14] Daniel Tack, of Game Informer, gave the game a score of 7/10; finding the boss battles enjoyable, but insufficient; and the game's level design bland and repetitive, describing it as a "sleepy, automated button mash".[12] Michael Huber, from GameTrailers, gave the game a 7.9/10, praising the four distinct classes while also criticizing the lack of varied environment and replay value.[13]
