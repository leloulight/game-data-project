World of Warships (formerly called World of Battleships) is an upcoming massively multiplayer online game being produced by Wargaming. The game will be similar to World of Tanks and World of Warplanes, having several types of military ships fulfilling different roles.

Wargaming announced that beta testing for World of Warships will begin sometime in the first quarter of 2015.

Early screenshots show that British, American, Soviet Union, German and Japanese ships will be in the game and the developers have confirmed the future addition of ships from other nations. Wargaming has only confirmed the release of the American and Japanese fleets in the Alpha version of the game and say they will add the German, French, Italian, British, and Soviet Union navies later on.

History[edit]

On August 16, 2011, the company website of Wargaming.net, developer and publisher of World of Tanks and World of Warplanes, announced a new game called World of Battleships, a free-to-play naval action MMO. It is intended to complete the "World of" war trilogy developed by the company.[1]

On August 2, 2012, the game was renamed to World of Warships.[2]

On November 14, 2013, the game entered closed alpha testing.[3]

During the 2014 edition of the Tokyo Gameshow, Wargaming CEO Victor Kislyi announced a collaboration between his company's online naval strategy game World of Warships and the Arpeggio of Blue Steel anime at Tokyo Game Show 2014 on Thursday. Wargaming is revealing more details in this collaboration during the stage event at its booth on Thursday.[4]

Wargaming already collaborated with another anime, Girls und Panzer, for its World of Tanks game, and Arpeggio of the Blue Steel already collaborated with another japanese popular naval game, Kantai Collection.

Gameplay[edit]

Battle Groups[edit]

Battle groups are the equivalent of flights in World of Warplanes and platoons in World of Tanks, allowing a group of players to join and fight battles together.

Location[edit]

Battles will take place on specific maps, which are set places with different weather conditions. Wargaming.net has said that maps will take place in the Atlantic, Pacific, and Arctic. Each map will have its own "personality" and flow of battle.

Technology[edit]

The technology based on World of Warships is that there will be light cruisers, heavy cruisers, battleships, aircraft carriers, destroyers, and battle cruisers. These ships can be either real ships or prototypes from a variety of forces such as the Imperial Japanese Navy, Kriegsmarine, US Navy, Royal Navy, and the Soviet Navy. Submarines and torpedo boats will not be present in World of Warships.

Clan Wars[edit]

Clan wars in World of Warship will be played out in the ocean. Clan Wars is where different clans play a "special game" in order to get land or maintain control of a region. Like World of Tanks and later World of Warplanes, different sectors of the ocean can be occupied by different clans and ports that enables for ships to get out on the front.

Financial System[edit]

Credits are the main in-game currency in the World of Warships arsenal. They enable players to buy ships, shells, and mount equipment on their ships. Similar to World of Tanks and World of Warplanes, special premium ships will be available for purchase with in-game currency (called 'gold'). Players can also use in-game currency to purchase a 'Premium Account' which gives them a 50% increase for experience (xp) and credits (silver) earned in all matches, as well as other benefits.
