Botanicula is a point-and-click adventure game developed by Amanita Design. The game was released on April 19, 2012, for Microsoft Windows, OS X and Linux. A version for iOS will also be released on May 1, 2014. The game is also in consideration to be ported to Android.[4] Botanicula is helping the World Land Trust to protect hundreds of acres of rainforest.[5]

Plot summary[edit]

Botanicula follows the adventure of five botanical creatures (named Mr. Lantern, Mr. Twig, Mr. Poppy Head, Mr. Feather and Mrs. Mushroom) who are trying to save the last seed of their home tree from evil parasitic creatures that have infested their home.

Characters[edit]

  • Mr. Lantern - The main protagonist of Botanicula. He is a small orange creature. He can absorb seeds within his body. It makes him glow and gives him power.
  • Mrs. Mushroom - The second playable character. She is the only female member of group. She can create identical copies of herself. Another power that she has is to shrink.
  • Mr. Poppyhead - The most robust member of the group and the third playable character.
  • Mr. Twig - Another playable character. He resembles a twig. He has many arms that he can expand. He also has ability to spurt flowers.
  • Mr. Feather - The last playable character. He is very small but can fly.
  • Spiders - Antagonists in the game. They attacked the tree that is the home of main characters.

Gameplay[edit]

Like the previous games from Amanita Design, in Botanicula the player controls a protagonist (or multiple protagonists) while exploring the environment, solving puzzles and collecting items that are required for the obstacles that come in their way. The player is able to interact with other creatures and find easter eggs that are present in the lush environment.

Like Machinarium, the game has no spoken or written dialogue.[6]

  • Mr. Lantern, Mrs. Mushroom, Mr. Poppy Head, Mr. Feather and Mr. Twig

  • Jaromír Plachý, designer of Botanicula, and Jakub Dvorský, Amanita Design founder

  • The band DVA created all music and sounds of Botanicula

Awards[edit]

Botanicula won the 14th annual award in 'Excellence in Audio' at Independent Games Festival 2012.[7] The game was also a showcased nominee and received the Story/World Design award at IndieCade (the International Festival of Independent Games) in 2012.[8] Botanicula also in European Games Award 2012 in category Best European Adventure Game.[9]

Botanicula was elected in Booom competition to be the Best Czech video game of 2012.[10] The game also appeared on Anifilm where it was awarded byt Czech video game of the year Award for artistic contribution to Czech video game output in 2012.[11]

Critical reception[edit]

The game received overwhelmingly positive reviews from critics.[12][13][14][15] It was praised for its music and visuals, as well as gameplay. However, other reviews noted a lack of replay value.[16][17][18]
