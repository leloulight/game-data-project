Blade Symphony is a multiplayer action video game developed by Puny Human Games. It was released for Microsoft Windows on May 7, 2014[3][4][5] and is currently available on Steam.

Gameplay[edit]

Blade Symphony places emphasis on swordplay with an in-depth sword fighting system similar to the Jedi Knight series. The game features multiple versus modes, such as deathmatch or 2 vs 2.

Development[edit]

Early History[edit]

The concept for Blade Symphony began in 2006 when lead designer Michael Chang and his friend wanted to create a sword-fighting game in the vein of the saber dueling found in Jedi Knight II: Jedi Outcast: "In 2006 a friend and I were sitting in the living room, super bored. We're saying to ourselves, 'Look, Jedi Knight 2: Jedi Outcast was a great game. It happened, but people stopped playing it. We really need something to scratch the sword fighting itch that we have."[6]

Chang and his friend began work on the game in the form of a Source engine total-conversion mod until 2008 when his friend dropped out of the project. Chang continued to work on the project and looked for talent on modding forums for help on the project. Eventually, a team of modders behind the popular Source engine modification Dystopia expressed interest in his project and wanted to acquire his development team. The team eventually came together to form the independent game developer Puny Human.

Kickstarter Campaign[edit]

In June 2011, the developers launched a Kickstarter campaign to raise money for a Source engine license.[7][8] The game achieved its $15,000 funding goal within 7 days.[9]
