Angry Birds Stella, which has the slogan "best friends forever – most of the time", is a puzzle video game and the second spin-off from the Angry Birds series, developed by Rovio Entertainment. It is based around Stella, the pink bird, and her adventures with her friends. The game is being marketed to females, but appeals to all sexes.[1]

Announced on February 13, 2014, Rovio stated that the game would be accompanied by a toy-line with Telepods, and a cartoon series.[2][3] The game was released on September 4, 2014.[4]

Gameplay[edit]

Just as in the original Angry Birds, players use a slingshot to launch an assortment of birds at nearby structures, with the intent of hitting targets located on or within them.

Stella and her friends team up to defeat Gale, the bad princess who stole Stella's island map and her pictures.

Telepods[edit]

Like Angry Birds Star Wars II and Angry Birds Go!, Angry Birds Stella features Telepods,[5] first seen at Toy Fair 2014. Telepods are figures created by Hasbro, that each have a QR code on the bottom which, when scanned, will summon a character into the game. Some Telepod packs may contain toy pieces, to "dress up" the characters or to shoot them at the pigs. This game is the 3rd Rovio title to include Telepods

Television[edit]

Rovio announced an animated series based on the game, with the first episode scheduled to be released on November 1, 2014 on ToonsTV.[6]

Comics[edit]

Rovio confirmed, during San Diego Comic-Con 2014, that IDW Publishing will be making an Angry Birds Stella comic book series.[citation needed]

Reception[edit]

The game has received generally mixed reviews with a Metacritic score of 67/100 based on 8 reviews.[7] Pocket Gamer UK said, "It's Angry Birds. If you care about that any more then you'll probably find something to enjoy. Just watch out for the timers."[8] Some reviewers have praised the game for it's introduction of new characters and return to the original style of gameplay.[9]
