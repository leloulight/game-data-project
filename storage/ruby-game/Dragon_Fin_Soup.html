Dragon Fin Soup is an action role-playing video game created by the independent development studio Grimm Bros. The game is the studio's first title and is planned for release on Microsoft Windows, OS X, Linux, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 3, and PlayStation Vita.[1] It is influenced by roguelike games and uses procedural generation for parts of the game. The game has two modes: Story mode follows a story and is more like a typical RPG, while Survival mode skips the story and focuses on the surviving within the game and constrains players with permadeath—once the player character dies, the game must be restarted from the beginning.[2]

Gameplay[edit]

Screenshot from the game

Dragon Fin Soup is a role-playing game with roguelike qualities and graphics inspired by the Super Nintendo, playing from a top-down, 2-dimensional perspective.[3] Tactical combat[4] and movement take place in a turns, with both the player and enemies taking actions during each turn.[3] The game includes scripted events, procedurally generated content, and randomly generated quests to complete.[1] Two modes of play are available: Story mode has the player controlling the first playable character, Red Robin, as she works her way through a pre-scripted storyline in the vein of a typical role-playing game.[1] Survival mode starts players out in a forest where they must find supplies and fight through increasingly difficult randomly-generated dungeons. Survival mode also includes permadeath, so if the player character dies during the game then the game starts over completely.[1]

A range of activities can be pursued including fishing, crafting, gathering, mining, digging, and cooking.[3][4] Players have 20 slots in which to store equipment and other useful items,[3] are able to dual-wield weapons,[3] and can acquire pets to join them in the game.[4] The world includes many different locations to explore including forests, caves, mines, deserts, mansions, castles, and ruins.[5] The climate in the game is randomly generated which affects both the weather and earthquakes in each area.[5]

Plot[edit]

The game takes place on Asura, a world situated on the back of an enormous dragon-turtle.[1] The story follows Red Robin, an alcoholic yet cheerful mercenary.[3] The Story mode for the game follows a line of quests which reveal the game's lore.[1] The game uses elements from fairy tales including Robin Hood[3] and Little Red Riding Hood.[4]

Development[edit]

Concept art for the main character

The game is the first title by the five person independent development studio Grimm Bros.[2][3] The development studio was founded in March 2013 by Human Hand's former COO Ash Montif and by Randis Albion, an artist that previously worked on League of Legends, AquaNox: The Angel's Tears, and Magic: The Gathering.[1][4]

The developers posted the game on Kickstarter, a website used to help fund the costs of the development by taking pre-orders for the game. The campaign started with an initial goal of US$24,000 to provide funding the base game. By March 21, 2014, the campaign reached their initial goal. The developers also included stretch goals in the campaign which, if reached, would provide funding for expansions to the game as well as cover the costs of porting the additions to the Sony gaming platforms.[1] As part of a 75-game wave of approvals, Valve Corporation accepted the game to be sold and distributed through their Steam software platform.[1]

Reception[edit]

Before the game's release, Eurogamer's Robert Purchese described the art style as "colorful, eye-catching and visually charming".[3]
