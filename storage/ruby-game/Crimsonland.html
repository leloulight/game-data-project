Crimsonland is a top-down computer game with RPG elements.[1] It was developed by 10tons Entertainment and published by Reflexive Entertainment in 2003.[2][3]

Gameplay[edit]

Upon the beginning player is placed in the middle of a game location with a weapon, health points and zero experience. The screen is gradually filled with different monsters. Getting a large amount of kills, character earns experience, levels up and gets rewarded with perks such as faster reloading, extra speed and so on.[4][5]

Game modes[edit]

The game features four game modes. In Quest mode, player wins if he overcomes a large number of monsters. In Rush mode, gamer gains victory once he manages to survive as long as possible fighting off crowds of monsters. Survival mode fields a mixture of two previous scenarios: player kills monsters and acquires experience points to obtain perks.[6] In Typ’o’Shooter mode player can't move. Monsters appear with written words above them. In order to shoot slowly advancing aliens, player has to type out tricky words and odd combinations.[5]

Bonuses[edit]

  • Double Experience. Doubles experience for all kills.
  • Energizer. Turns monsters blue: when player runs over them, they die giving player health points.
  • Fireblast. Spays out plasma bullets.
  • Fire Bullets. Enables to shoot with fire bullets.
  • Freeze. Encases monster with ice.
  • MediKit. Gives player about 10% of maximum health.
  • Reflex Boost. Slows time allowing player to consider his actions carefully.
  • Shield. Makes player immune to the attacks.
  • Shock Chain. Unleashes a ricocheting ion bolt that bounces among monsters.
  • Speed. Doubles player's run speed.
  • Weapon Power Up. Doubles the firing rate and increases the reloading process.[7]
