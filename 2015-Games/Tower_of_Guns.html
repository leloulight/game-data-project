Tower of Guns is a roguelike Single-player first-person shooter video game developed by Terrible Posture Games. It features levels that are randomly created from a pool of possible elements, making each playthrough unique.[1]

The game was officially released on March 4, 2014 to generally positive reviews.[2]

Gameplay[edit]

Tower of Guns is a first-person shooter that takes place in a tower filled with a variety of guns. The player must ascend the tower by advancing through as series randomized areas. Each area contains a number of arenas and a boss fight that the player must clear to advance.[3]

At the outset of the game, the player may choose one gun and one perk before they enter the tower. The tower is filled with randomly placed robots and sentry turrets that fire projectiles at the player. These projectiles can produce a variety of effects, such as area of effect damage, fire damage, or launching the player into the air. When an enemy is destroyed, blue tokens are produced that the player can pick up to increase their gun's level. The tower also contains various power ups that alter gameplay and up-gradable guns that the player can unlock.[3][4]

The game is intended to be completed within one to two hours, and has been described as a "lunch-break experience."[4][5]

Development[edit]

Joe Mirabello, the founder and sole member of Terrible Posture Games, began production on Tower of Guns after the collapse of 38 Studios, where he had been working on Project Copernicus, an unreleased massively multiplayer online game based on Kingdoms of Amalur. Mirabello said that "after working on a game with hundreds of people made for thousands of people, I wanted to work on a single-player game made by just one person." A pre-alpha version of the game debuted at IndieCade booth at the 2013 Electronic Entertainment Expo.[6]

The completed build of the game was released on March 4, 2014 through a number of online clients, including Steam, GOG.com, GamersGate and IndieGameStand.

Reception[edit]
