Rise of Incarnates is an upcoming multiplayer free-to-play fighting video game published by Bandai Namco Entertainment. Announced early 2014 and presented at the 2014 Electronic Entertainment Expo, the game features two-on-two gameplay. The game is developed by the same staff that worked on the Soul, Tekken and Mobile Suit Gundam: Extreme Vs. series, three major fighting game franchises of Bandai Namco. It is scheduled to be released later in 2015.[1]

Gameplay[edit]

The game's playable characters are the so-called Incarnates, humans who have awoken mythical powers. Players could apply long-range assault with close-quarter combat. Their powers allow them to transform into a creature or summon a being to assist them in battle.[2]

Battle arenas are set in places like New York, San Francisco, London and Paris.

Plot[edit]

Rise of Incarnates is set in a world suffering from catastrophic events dubbed as the "cube phenomena": meteorites falling from the sky and massive cold fronts sweeping across the land have led the people in the planet to live in a state of perpetual fear. Among the people are those known as "incarnates"—humans who can draw powers from daimones; spirit manifestations of the gods, demons, and monsters from all of the world's mythologies. General public fear incarnates for their supernatural powers, while the authorities attempt to harness their powers for their own deeds. A battle of dominance between each Incarnates is triggered when one day, an enigmatic voice speaks in the mind of every incarnates, requiring them to defeat the "Sovereign of this era" to avoid a certain destruction.[1]

Characters[edit]

So far, six characters have been announced with more to be unveiled at a later date:[1][3]

  • Jedrek Tyler
    A young man who has recently awakened his Incarnate after a street fight that left him severely injured, whose Mephistopheles often enters Jedrek's mind and urges him to succumb to his thirst for violence and devastation. His daimon is Mephistopheles.
  • Terrence Blake
    A member of a U.S. government organization intending to stop the persecution of Incarnates, who has the power of "Deuteros" (the ability to summon a god to aid him). His daimon is Ares.
  • Mireia Valentin
    An arms dealer eager to venture on the highest classified profile in the U.S. government known as "the Prophet" and whose Incarnate transforms her into a multi-winged creature. Her daimon is Lilith.
  • Gasper Watteu
    A specialist in Incarnate research-turned-criminal wanting to exact revenge against the U.S. government for having abandoned his project whose Incarnate allows him to summon zombies at his disposal. His daimon is the Grim Reaper.
  • Erendira Quinn
    Commander of the Incarnate-persecuting Wild Hunt organization with an abusive childhood who has deep hatred towards rogue Incarnates. Her only loyalty is to President Sanders, even when his plans are in question. Her daimon is Brynhildr.
  • Reinhold Kruger
    A retired general wanting to overture the European power structure and heighten the Incarnates' dominance. His daimon is Odin.
  • Ricardo Abascal
    Former doctor who used to hate incarnates after his family was killed by one of them, but after saving a healer boy's life and becoming incarnate through the child's power, he cast that hatred aside and promised to help anyone along the way, considering both sides as equal from now on. His daimon is Ra.
  • Brad Burrell
    A member of the Were-Legion forces who used to think he had important mission until Colonel Kruger took charge. From then on, Burrell owes his loyalty to him. His daimon is Fenrir.
  • Fernando Duran / The Slayer
    A follower of Akohr who was subjected to an experiment which his human body was modified with an armor and a lamina weapon, giving him the ability to control his own lamina energy.
  • Zaur Miljkovich
    An ex-employee of Akohf who went on the run after awaking his incarnate, until he was found by the Vikarāla corporation. His daimon is Loki..
  • Asha Mehta
    The ruthless CEO of Vikarāla Corporation who is known because of her work in the post-disaster global reconstruction effort. She possesses a vision of the future in which incarnates reign over the powerless masses. Her followers call her Rani ("lady"). Her daimon is Kali.
  • President Sanders
    The President of the United States who ascended to the presidency placing an incarnate at the pinnacle of American power. He is the true leader of the Wild Hunt faction, and has a prisoner called "The Prophet" in custody but he needs to capture "The Herald" in order to archive his final goal. His daimon is Zeus.

Supporting characters[edit]

  • Oswald Coleman / The Prophet
    The person is considerably responsible for bringing each incarnate his/her own Lamina weapon in order to defeat the Sovereign. He is currently being kept as a prisoner by the U.S. government lead by President Sanders.
  • Emily
    Jedrek Tyler's girlfriend who has no fear against him an an incarnate. After they crashed within gang-controlled territory, Jedrek unleashed Mephistopheles for the first time. Pursued by the Wild Hunt thereafter, referring her as the "Herald". Though not an incarnate per se, she appears to have the ability to gather and channel Lamina energy.
  • Kanat
    A healer boy who is one of Akhor's targets, and he is being safely kept by Ricardo Abascal.

Comics[edit]

The game's official website is currently releasing an online graphic novel that features some of the game's events.[4] In collaboration with Marvel Comics, Bandai Namco adapted the web comic into a 16-inch comic book.[5]

The new comic series made its debut on July 23, 2014 at San Diego Comic-Con, with the first two issues in a combined volume. Marvel Custom Solutions vice president Jonathan Rheingold said "Bandai Namco Games and Marvel working together was a no-brainer. Both companies hold to the highest standards of quality storytelling, artistic talent recruitment and character creation, so it was a natural connection for Marvel to offer its expertise to the legendary video game developer in packaging and distributing the comic book adaptation of Rise of Incarnates."

The newly-released volume will also be available at approximately 2,500 comic speciality shops supporting Marvel Comics for free from July 24, 2014. Additionally, the comics will be accessible for free digitally through the Marvel Unlimited Comics App on iOS and Android devices.
