Furry Legends is a video game franchise developed by Gamelion Studios available on WiiWare and on alternative mobile platforms. The game was released in North America on July 19, 2010, and in PAL regions on August 20, 2010.[1] The game costs 1000 Wii Points.

Gameplay[edit]

Players can control Furballs by using the Wii Remote and Nunchuk. Using the analogue stick on the Nunchuk makes Furballs rolling to the left and right and control their flight a bit. The A button makes character jumping. Pressing Z button, choosing direction and shaking the Wii Remote allows to attack Sqauries and other objects. Every Furball has the unique abilities that can be released by B button when the character's level of FurPower is high enough.

Development[edit]

The development process was revealed by the team in many interviews done by websites interested in the gaming industry.[2][3][4][5]

Other platforms[edit]

The game has been published by SkyZone Entertainment in Verizon Wireless network for BREW and Android platforms [6] and on Android Market. Furry Legends for Samsung Bada was officially announced on February 15, 2010 at the Mobile World Congress in Barcelona. The game is based on the 3D version of Furry Legends and scheduled for launch in 2010.[7] All platforms: Androit Appel Dsiware Wiiware

While initial previews were somewhat positive, overall reception for the title was mixed.[15] Currently on Metacritic, Furry Legends holds a score of 61% citing mixed or average reviews.[8] IGN awarded the game a score of 6 out of 10, criticising the length, physics and game-play.[12] Similarly, Cheat Code Central awarded the game a score of 2 out of 5, noting that the game was more frustrating than fun, and stating that "[While] there are some chestnuts to be found, they lay buried beneath a mass of platforming desecration." [14]
