Rise of Incarnates is an upcoming multiplayer free-to-play fighting video game published by Bandai Namco Games. Announced early 2014 and presented at the 2014 Electronic Entertainment Expo, the game features two-on-two gameplay. The game is developed by the same staff that worked on the Soul, Tekken and Mobile Suit Gundam: Extreme Vs. series, three major fighting game franchises of Bandai Namco. It is scheduled to be released later in 2014.[1]

Plot[edit]

Rise of Incarnates is set in a world suffering from catastrophic events dubbed as the "cube phenomena": meteorites falling from the sky and massive cold fronts sweeping across the land have led the people in the planet to live in a state of perpetual fear. Among the people are those known as "incarnates"-- humans who can draw powers from daimones; spirit manifestations of the gods, demons, and monsters from all of the world's mythologies. General public fear incarnates for their supernatural powers, while the authorities attempt to harness their powers for their own deeds. A battle of dominance between each Incarnates is triggered when one day, an enigmatic voice speaks in the mind of every incarnates, requiring them to defeat the "Sovereign of this era" to avoid a certain destruction.[1]

Characters[edit]

The game's playable characters are the so-called Incarnates, humans who can draw powers from daimones; spiritual manifestations of various gods, demons, and monsters from mythologies around the world. The powers allow them to transform into a creature or summon a being to assist them in battle.[2]

So far, six characters have been announced with more to be unveiled at a later date:[1] Dr. Gasper Watteu (Grim Reaper), a specialist in Incarnate research-turned-criminal wanting to exact revenge against the U.S. government for having abandoned his project whose Incarnate allows him to summon zombies at his disposal; Terrence Blake (Ares), a member of a U.S. government organization intending to stop the persecution of Incarnates, who has the power of "Deuteros": the ability to summon a being (Ares) to aid him; Mireia Valentin (Lilith), an arms dealer eager to venture on the highest classified profile in the U.S. government known as "the Prophet" and whose Incarnate transforms her into a multi-winged creature; Jedrek Tyler (Mephistopheles), a young man who has recently awakened his Incarnate after a street fight that left him severely injured, whose Mephistopheles often enters Jedrek's mind and urges him to succumb to his thirst for violence and devastation; Erendira Quinn (Brynhildr), commander of the Incarnate-persecuting Wild Hunt organization with an abusive childhood who has deep hatred towards rogue Incarnates; Reinhold Kruger (Odin), retired general wanting to overture the European power structure and heighten the Incarnates' dominance; and Ricardo Abascal (Ra), former doctor who used to hate incarnates after his family was killed by one of them, but after saving a healer boy's life and becoming incarnate through the child's power, he casted that hatred aside and promised to help anyone along the way, considering both sides as equal from now on.
