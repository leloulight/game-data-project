Teslagrad is a 2D side scrolling puzzle platformer developer and published by Rain Games. The game was released and published on Steam on December 13, 2013[1] and Nintendo Wii U on September 11, 2014.[2] The game is slated for launch on Playstation 3[3] and Playstation Vita[4] later in 2014. Later, Rain Game announced that the game is headed for PlayStation 4 alongside a retail release of the game.[5] The game uses Unity game engine, making it easier for developers to reach cross platform.[6]

Gameplay[edit]

Teslagrad is a 2D puzzle platformer with action elements, where magnetism and other electromagnetic powers are the key to go throughout the game, and thereby discover the secrets kept in the long abandoned Tesla Tower. The game features minimal combat/action segment on the other hand coupled with electric based puzzle section and precise platforming. The game features a non-linear world with more than 100 beautiful hand-drawn environments, in a steampunk-inspired vision of old Europe.

In-game screenshot showing colourful hand drawn art style.

You play as a young boy who suddenly finds himself embroiled in a long-forgotten conspiracy, involving the despotic king who has ruled the nation with an iron fist for several years. The game offers various upgrades and items as the game progresses. The game features an on hit kill which has often been criticised by various critics.[7]

History[edit]

Teslagrad was originally conceived as the world of Chroma evolved, derived from our casual-focused multiplayer game called Minute Mayhem. Rain Games stopped the development of the first game that the team was working on in order to focus on Teslagrad. Wii U[8] and PlayStation 3[3] versions of the game were announced on June and July 2013, respectively.

The game saw the launch of first playable demo for the PC was released on August 2013. Later that year, developers Rain Game showcased Teslagrad at various indie game events such as PAX Prime 2013 and Eurogamer Expo 2013, gathering invaluable feedback and being warmly received by players and press. The game uses Unity game engine, making it easier for developers to reach cross platform.[6]

Plot[edit]

In the Kingdom of Elektropia, a king rules with an iron fist, combating and destroying a sect of technological wizards who have a massive tower in the middle of the city named Teslagrad. One day, a young orphaned boy finds himself hounded by the King's guards, fleeing into the deserted tower and uncovering the history of the conflict.

The game has received positive reviews amongst all platform. Nintendo Life praised the game for its nostalgic music, hand drawn artwork and brain bending puzzle.[14] Nintendo World Report also praised the game for its art style, however, they criticised the game for its steep difficulty and minimal Wii U Gamepad usage.[7]
