Loadout is a free-to-play third person shooter developed by Austin-based studio, Edge of Reality. Currently only available to download on Steam, Loadout focuses on arcade-style multi-player firefights across a variety of modes. The game is notable for its over-the-top cartoon gore and wealth of character customization options. Players can alter the appearance of their in-game avatar as well as the properties of their weapons.[1]

Gamemodes[edit]

There are five types of games: Death Snatch, Blitz, Jackhammer, Annihilation, and Extraction, most of which are based around collecting Blutonium, a fictional element.[2]

Death Snatch: Upon killing an enemy player, the dead player will drop a vial of Blutonium which can be grabbed to add to team score.

Extraction: Extraction is a game mode in which the first team to win is the one to collect the most Blutonium, an object in the game that rewards one team with points. A player who is designated as the Collector can travel around, picking up Blutonium and depositing it in his/her team's basket, gaining points for his/her team. The Collectors are chosen randomly from the currently alive players.

Blitz: The map is dotted with poles, and every so often, one will be designated as a control point. Teams will compete for control of the point, as standing near it slowly raises a team-colored pair of boxers, as well as taking down the enemy's boxers. When the boxers reach the top of the flag, the point is captured and it is added to the team score.

Jackhammer: Jackhammer is a gamemode similar to Capture The Flag, except you can kill people with the flag. Your goal is to take the enemy's hammer and return it to your base. When the enemy's hammer is in your control, you can smash people with it, killing them instantly. Each hammer has 5 smashes per capture. Capturing the hammer will add to the team score.

Annihilation: Annihilation is a game mode that combines elements from all other game modes. The goal is to get a team score of 10,000. Several things can add to your team score. Players drop Blutonium vials on death, similar to Death Snatch. The vials can be picked up to gain team score points. As well, there are two control points which can be captured to gain a temporary damage buff, and adding to the team score, similar to Blitz. There is also a Jackhammer, similar to Jackhammer, which can be captured and used to kill people. Adding to the team score will also give the player a growing supply of personal Blutonium, which can be used to purchase upgrades for your character. There are three types of Upgrades. The first is attack, which buff the amount of damage you do, the second is Tank, which adds the amount of damage you can withstand, and Health, which increases the healing you can dish out and also adds to the effectiveness of Health packs. Once a team has reached 10,000 points, the team must take the enemy's hammer back to their base, and charge it. Then, the team must take the hammer back to the enemy's base, and smash it inside the base, destroying it, finally winning the match.

Domination: Domination is a game mode where you capture points, and each team starts at 0 points. You get 20 points when you capture a point and 5 per second while the point is captured. Each team must capture the points once it is captured by the other team. If there is a red arrow under the point at the top of the screen, the other team is capping the point.

Campaign: The campaign isn't available yet but it's known that up to four players can play cooperatively against an alien threat.[3]

Loadout's weaponcrafting makes it a unique game, allowing you to craft weapons to your taste. Lots of outfit options are available as well, so you stand out from others.

Loadout received mixed reviews,currently holding a 72 on Metacritic,[11] which indicates "Mixed or Average Reviews". IGN gave Loadout a 6.9 but stated in their review: "The matchmaking system is straight-up broken, leading to frequent frustrating mismatches, and its four maps aren't enough to keep me around for long."[12] PC Gamer also gave the game a 77 & said in their review "Loadout's gun customization and well executed twists on shooter staples make it a free-to-play game that doesn't feel like a compromise."[13]
