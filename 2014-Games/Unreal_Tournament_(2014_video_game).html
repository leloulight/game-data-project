Unreal Tournament is an upcoming first-person shooter video game developed by Epic Games.[8] It is part of the Unreal series of games, and is a sequel to 2007's Unreal Tournament 3. The game utilizes Epic's Unreal Engine 4 and will be released for free on Windows, OS X, and Linux.[9][10]

Unreal Tournament is unique in that the development will be crowdsourced and open to contribution from anyone.[11][12]

Development[edit]

Unreal Tournament was teased on May 2, 2014, when Mark Rein, vice president and co-founder of Epic Games, stated on Twitter that Unreal Tournament is back, stating, "I love UnrealTournament - So excited for the comeback! More next week!".[13][14][15] Paul Meegan, vice president of product development at Epic, followed Rein's tweet with information about the future of Unreal Tournament.[16][17][18] The game was announced by Epic in a Twitch livestream on May 8, 2014.[9][19] During the livestream, senior programmer and project lead Steve Polge said that there had been demand for a reboot of the Unreal Tournament and that the release of the Unreal Engine 4, made this the ideal time to do so. He said, "For years, weâ€™ve wanted to reboot Unreal Tournament, but we knew we had to do it in concert with developers and the mod community, and in an environment that sets them up with the proper tools to make it happen. Given the recent launch of UE4, we think this is the right time to move forward."[20]

Development on the game began on May 8, 2014, on the same day of the announcement.[10] Unreal Tournament is being developed using Unreal Engine 4 in the open in close collaboration between Epic Games and the community, "anyone with an Unreal Engine 4 subscription can participate in its development", Epic said.[8] On August 15, 2014, Epic released a playable pre-alpha, however it is in no way representative of the end goal and the completed game as it comes with untextured maps, placeholders, bugs, unstable weaponry and other things that change quickly since it is still in pre-alpha phase.[21][22] Originally, the pre-alpha was only available to UE4 subscribers, but the way the game is being developed made it possible to compile Epic's builds and release them to the public.[23][24][25][26] Therefore, Epic Games made it available to Unreal Tournament forum members for Windows, Mac and Linux, and will be getting weekly updates.[27]

On September 5, 2014, Epic hosted an Unreal Tournament event, where several competitive players and enthusiasts from each generation of UT as well as some of the community contributors, were invited to play the early prototype version of the game at Epic's headquarters in Cary, North Carolina.[28] The event was meant for providing feedback on the gameplay.[29][30]
