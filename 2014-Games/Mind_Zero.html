Mind Zero, stylized MIND≒0,[2] (Japanese: マインド/ゼロ, Hepburn: Maindo/Zero?) is a role-playing video game developed for the PlayStation Vita portable video game console. It was developed jointly by Acquire and Class of Heroes developer ZeroDiv, and published by GungHo Online Entertainment. It was released in Japan on August 1, 2013, and in May 2014 in Europe and North America by Aksys Games.[1]

Gameplay[edit]

The premise of the game has received many comparisons to the Persona series of role-playing video games.[3][4][5] The game's dialogue will be fully voiced and take place in 2D, similar to that of a visual novel, while the games battle system will consist of 3D computer graphics and turn-based battles.[4]

Story[edit]

The game will take place in two different "worlds"; the real world, and a "crazy spiritual world".[4] The real world aspect of the game is set in Japan, in the regions Akihabara, Yokohama, Amamiya and Nippori.[6] Specifically, the game takes place in the Saitama Prefecture.[3]

Development[edit]

The game was first announced in March 2013 in an issue of Famitsu.[6] It was announced to be in development jointly by Acquire and Class of Heroes developer ZeroDiv.[7] As of announcement, the game was reported to be 70% complete.[2] The game was scheduled for release in August 1, 2013 in Japan, and was the first game released by publisher GungHo Online Entertainment since buying out Acquire in 2012.[7] After the existence of a pre-order spot for the game was noted at websites such as GameStop in January 2014,[8] Aksys Games formally announced the game for a physical and digital release in North America, and a digital-only release in Europe, on the 30th of January.[1] The game was later greenlit[9] via the Steam Greenlight voting system and will be available for PC via Steam.

Reception[edit]

Upon release, Mind Zero has received mixed reception. Hardcore Gamer gave the game a 4/5, saying "For a game without any kind of established lineage, this is a very welcome addition to the Vita’s growing JRPG library.",[10] whereas IGN gave Mind Zero a 7/10, praising the title for its battle system and voice acting but criticized it for repetitive dungeons and poor writing.[11] GameSpot was much more critical with a 4.0/10, citing repetitive gameplay and that the game "fails at anything beyond being occasionally mediocre.".[13]
